Guide 1 · Beginners
Getting started in 60 seconds

TotTaps requires no download, no login, and no setup. Here's all you need to do:

  1. Open TotTaps in any web browser on your phone, tablet or computer.
  2. Tap "Start Tapping — It's Free" on the landing screen. The app will ask to go fullscreen — allow it.
  3. Hand the device to your toddler. Every key press, tap or click now creates a colourful explosion on screen.
  4. Letters A–Z speak their name out loud. Numbers 0–9 are read as words. Special keys show fun illustrations.
  5. To exit, hold the top-left corner for 2 seconds to open the parent panel, then tap "Exit Fullscreen".
💡
Pro tip: Bookmark TotTaps on your home screen so you can open it instantly next time — no typing required.

Guide 2 · Device Setup
Enabling fullscreen & kiosk mode

TotTaps goes fullscreen automatically when you tap Start. For extra protection against exits, use your device's built-in kiosk features:

  1. iPad — Guided Access: Go to Settings → Accessibility → Guided Access → turn it on. Open TotTaps, triple-click the side button, tap Start. Your child can't leave the app.
  2. Android — Screen Pinning: Go to Settings → Security → Screen Pinning → turn it on. Open TotTaps, tap the recent apps button, then tap the pin icon.
  3. Laptop / Desktop: TotTaps uses the browser's native Fullscreen API. Press F11 or use the fullscreen button in the parent panel for a clean locked experience.
  4. Chromebook: Press the fullscreen key (F4) or use the parent panel button. Works well for nursery and classroom use.
⚠️
Note: Some browsers on iOS don't support true fullscreen. For the best iPad experience, add TotTaps to your home screen and open it from there — it launches in a fuller view.

Guide 3 · Controls
Using the parent panel

The parent panel is hidden so your toddler can't accidentally open it. There are two ways to open it:

  1. Hold the top-left corner of the screen for 2 seconds. A settings icon appears — keep holding until the panel opens.
  2. Type the word "parent" on the keyboard. The panel opens as soon as the word is complete.

Inside the parent panel you can:

  1. Toggle sound on/off — useful for public places or when a nap is nearby.
  2. Reduce motion — turns off shaking and heavy effects for motion-sensitive children.
  3. Change theme — switch between Party, Jungle, Ocean, Space, Dino and Diwali themes.
  4. Set a session timer — choose 2, 5, 10 or 15 minutes. A progress bar counts down. When time is up, a fun Tap Report card appears.
  5. Reset the session — clears the tap count and restarts the timer if one is set.
  6. Exit fullscreen — safely returns to normal browser view.
💡
Timer tip: Setting a 5-minute timer and showing your toddler the countdown bar teaches them that screen time has a beginning and end — great for avoiding meltdowns when you need the device back.

Guide 6 · Development
Age-by-age guide
  1. 6–12 months — Sensory exploration
    Babies enjoy the cause-and-effect of pressing and seeing something happen. Use touch mode on a tablet. Any tap triggers colour bursts. Keep sessions to 2–3 minutes.
  2. 12–18 months — Cause and effect
    Toddlers begin to understand that they are making things happen. Point at the screen together. Say the letters as they appear. The Ocean or Party theme works well at this stage.
  3. 18 months–2 years — Letter & number recognition
    TotTaps speaks each letter and number aloud. Sit with your child and repeat what the app says. "A! Can you say A?" This age group often has a favourite key they press repeatedly — that's perfectly normal.
  4. 2–3 years — Keyboard exploration
    Children can now deliberately press keys to find specific illustrations. "Can you find the key that shows a car?" The chaos progression system (Calm → Wild → Legendary) keeps older toddlers engaged as sessions progress.
  5. 3–4 years — Pre-literacy play
    Older toddlers enjoy pressing letters they recognise from their name. Point out "that's the first letter of your name!" Use the Smash Report card at the end of sessions to celebrate their effort.

Guide 7 · Screen Time
Tips for healthy screen time balance

TotTaps is designed for short, supervised sessions — not hours of unsupervised use. Here's how to make it work well:

  1. Use the timer. Set a 5-minute limit in the parent panel. When the timer ends, the Tap Report card appears — a natural, positive ending to the session.
  2. Sit with your child. The most developmental benefit comes when a parent names what appears on screen. "Look, you made a butterfly!"
  3. Use it in context. TotTaps is perfect for waiting rooms, flights, or those 10 minutes you desperately need to finish something. Not as a daily babysitter.
  4. Follow your child's lead. If they lose interest after 2 minutes, that's fine. Don't extend the session. Short and joyful beats long and forced.
  5. Balance with physical play. After a TotTaps session, do a physical version — let your toddler bang on real pots and pans. The digital and physical versions of the same energy are both valuable.
💡
Screen time guidelines: The American Academy of Pediatrics recommends avoiding screen time for children under 18 months except for video calls. For 18–24 months, only high-quality content with a caregiver. For 2–5 years, limit to 1 hour per day of quality content. TotTaps sessions of 5–10 minutes with a parent present fit well within these guidelines.

Guide 8 · FAQ
Frequently asked questions
Is TotTaps completely free?
Yes. The core TotTaps experience — including all three free themes, all special key illustrations, sound, the parent panel, and session timer — is completely free with no ads and no account required.
Does TotTaps work offline?
Once loaded in your browser, TotTaps continues to work if you lose internet connection during a session. For fully offline use, add it to your home screen as a PWA on supported devices.
Can my toddler exit fullscreen accidentally?
TotTaps uses accidental-exit protection — the Escape key is disabled inside the app. For the strongest protection, use iPad Guided Access or Android Screen Pinning (see Guide 2 above).
Is the voice feature available on all devices?
The letter and number speech uses your device's built-in text-to-speech engine, which is available on all modern browsers and devices. Older or budget Android devices may have more limited voice options.
Will my child accidentally send emails or delete files?
No. Once TotTaps is in fullscreen mode, all keyboard inputs are captured by TotTaps and nothing is passed through to the operating system or other apps. Your files and emails are completely safe.

Can I use TotTaps in my nursery or classroom?
